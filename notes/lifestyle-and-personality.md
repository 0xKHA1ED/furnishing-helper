Deeper notes than `profile.json`. Updated as we talk. Both partners should be able to read this and say “yes, that’s us” or correct it.

## Core emotional brief (2026-07-17)

### Arrival feeling

**Recharge together — cozy shared nest.**  
After a long day, the home’s first job is **togetherness and warmth**, not solo recovery or pure systems. Design implications:

- Living room (or main lounge zone) is the **emotional center**, not a leftover after the kitchen  
- Soft light, comfortable seating for two that invites lingering  
- Avoid a house that only “works” but never invites sitting down together  
- Friday meal-prep is functional; evenings should still feel like a nest, not a canteen  

### Taste dynamic as a couple

**You (primary project driver) lean function; partner leans aesthetics.**  
This is a feature to design for, not a problem to ignore:

- Every major choice should pass **two gates**: *Does it work hard?* and *Does it feel right?*  
- Expensive items: document **function win** and **aesthetic win** separately so neither person feels overridden  
- Style direction should be clear enough for the aesthetics-led partner, practical enough that the function-led partner doesn’t feel trapped by fragile/pretty choices  
- **Conflict style:** not established yet — first big shared home. This project should *invent* a fair decision rule with you, not assume one  
- Working rule (draft): function veto on durability/maintenance/daily friction; aesthetics voice strong on anything you both see for hours (sofa, bed textiles, living light, color)  

### Meaning of home (early marriage)

**A stable base after the wedding whirlwind.**  
Home is grounding and continuity — adult life starting — more than a showpiece or pure productivity machine.

- Prefer **calm reliability** over trend-chasing  
- Phased purchases OK: stability first (sleep, food storage, clean clothes, hot water, soft landing place for two)  
- Styling should feel settled and intentional, not temporary dorm energy — even if budget is phased  

## Design principles derived (draft)

1. **Nest first** — prioritize the shared cozy center early among furniture spends  
2. **Dual gate** — aesthetics + function for big tickets  
3. **Stable base** — durability, warranty, timeless-enough looks over novelty  
4. **Prep-heavy kitchen** — systems for Friday batch cooking without making the whole home feel industrial  
5. **No AC** — comfort via textiles, shade, airflow, material choices  
6. **Rare hosting** — guest needs secondary; couple daily life primary (2nd room = guest/office hybrid, not formal guest suite)  

### Privacy vs family

**Mostly private nest — family is occasional.**  
Aligned with rare hosting. Implications:

- Do not oversize living for big family majlis energy  
- Guest sleep = second room hybrid, not a permanent formal guest wing  
- Durable enough for occasional visits, but daily layout optimizes **couple**  
- Soft boundaries: entry can feel calm/private rather than “receive everyone in the living room immediately” if you want later  

## Decision rule (draft — first shared home)

| Gate | Who watches it | Example |
|------|----------------|---------|
| Function / friction | You | Capacity, warranty, cleaning, noise, layout practicality |
| Aesthetics / feeling | Partner | Color, shape, textile, “does this feel like us?” |
| Shared lock | Both | Anything expensive or hard to reverse |

Neither gate alone ships a decision. If stuck: sleep on it, write both arguments in `data/decisions.json`, pick in a calm moment.
