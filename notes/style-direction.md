# Style direction (draft)

Not locked. Grows from personality talks in `lifestyle-and-personality.md`.

## North star (from couple brief)

| Pillar | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Cozy shared nest | Soft, inviting, together-focused living core |
| Stable base | Timeless, durable, unfussy — post-wedding grounding |
| Dual gate | Pretty enough for the aesthetics partner; robust/practical for the function partner |
| Prep-real kitchen | Honest working kitchen that doesn’t infect the whole home with “utility only” energy |

## Likely direction (hypothesis — confirm later)

- **Warm contemporary** rather than cold minimal or heavy classic  
- Soft textiles, layered warm lighting (no AC → blackout + breathable fabrics matter)  
- Clear surfaces where the function partner needs calm order; a few intentional aesthetic anchors where the other partner cares most  
- Avoid: fragile showroom pieces, ultra-trendy colors that age in 2 years, pure white sterile clinic look  

## Room hierarchy for “feeling like us”

1. Shared living / nest  
2. Master bedroom (rest as stability)  
3. Kitchen (high function, still not ugly)  
4. Second bedroom (hybrid — flexible, not over-designed)  
5. Entry / bath (simple, clean, welcoming enough)

## Social posture

**Private couple nest** — family occasional. Don’t design a reception house. Guest comfort is “good enough when needed,” not the main story.  

## Open style questions

- Warm neutrals vs more color personality  
- Wood/metal/fabric preferences  
- How much “Egyptian / local craft” vs global contemporary
